Şahsenem Saphira
Şahsenem Saphira( born 1st April 2000) is a German and Turkish singer and songwriter. She possesses a Mezzo-Soprano vocal range and is known for her ambiguous melancholic pop sound. After releasing her second single, "Flaws", she was discovered and instantly gained recognition for her soothing and seductive vocals.

While studying for her degree and gearing to become a healthcare professional, she built a portfolio as a model. She was featured on the cover of "Eighteen Magazine" in 2022.

Saphira's Multilingual Musical Tapestry

Diving into a multilingual realm, Saphira crafts enchanting melodies in the harmonious tongues of English, German, and Turkish. Her music becomes a vivid tapestry, celebrating the intricate beauty of these languages while delving into the rich tapestry of cultural diversity forged through her life's journey.

Early life

Saphira was born in Hamburg, Germany. Growing up in a family where the cultures could not be more different, she learned to speak German, Turkish, English, and Spanish.

Saphira's musical influence comes from her grandparents, who always listened to old-school music on their DVD player. After getting the gift of shimmy belt for her birthday, she would make her family be the audience to her choreographed music with dances at the age of 4.
Education

Saphira was in a musical high school in Turkey in 2016. She was taught to play the piano and learned ear-solfeggio training.

Meanwhile, her teachers told her parents about her exceptional performance in core subjects like writing, biology, and maths. The feedback prompted her parents to align her focus on studies, and drew her from music school. Her musical education halted during her school

Analogy Behind the Songs

Saphira released her first single, "Jim Carrey", which was rewarded in her faculty for its lyricism, like the politically provoking double meanings and the questioning attitude. In "Jim Carrey" Saphira, is slightly mentioning a few retrospective events:

1. "No way that this Angel look-alike Tarrant would ever do this." Şahsenem refers to the white supremacist Brenton Tarrant, who caused a terror attack in New Zealand in 2019 and killed 51 people during an anti-Muslim shooting in the Christchurch Mosque while the victims were praying.

2. "Choupette, may you give kids a slice of bread? Even though you are just a pet?" Saphira is mentioning "Choupette", the cat from Karl Lagerfeld, because he has left over 13 million $ of his fortune to his Cat, Choupette

3. "We have United Nations; why do you even worry? Or forgot the Srebrenica Massacre already?" Saphira is asking sarcastically whether the global organization United Nations could save all people from historical events like that, since they failed to prevent the Srebrenica Genocide in the year of 1995.

Mentions and Accolades

In April 2022, she released her second contemporary R&B single, "Flaws", and gained international attention and credits from curators and labels. Yet, she has not signed with any label.

Personal life

Saphira's first name, "Şahsenem", was given by her grandmother. It means "The most beautiful of beautiful women, a masterpiece" in Arabic. While her second name was given from her mother and means "beautiful" or "blue gem stone" in Hebrew.
